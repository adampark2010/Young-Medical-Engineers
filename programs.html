Three programs, one workshop

Free one-on-one tutoring.

A simple online form matches each student with a volunteer tutor — undergraduate and graduate students of medical and biomedical engineering, recruited through university programs and BMES chapters.

Request a tutor

What we cover

  • Biology, chemistry, physics, pre-calculus & calculus
  • Biomechanics, biomaterials, medical instrumentation
  • Anatomy & physiology
  • AP Biology · AP Physics · AP Chemistry
  • SAT / ACT science sections

Labs you can hold in your hands.

Low-cost, hands-on projects that make medical engineering tangible — run virtually with mailed kits or household materials, or in small in-person groups.

Host a lab

On the bench

  • Build a working stethoscope or pulse-oximeter circuit
  • Prototype a simple prosthetic hand — 3D-printed or cardboard
  • Assemble a DIY EKG / heart-rate sensor
  • Tissue-engineering demo on an agar or hydrogel scaffold
  • The Device Pitch — design a low-cost device, defend it live

Demonstrations that come to you.

Portable demo kits carried into classrooms, libraries, and community centers — grassroots outreach that meets students exactly where they already are.

Book a demo day

Out in the field

  • Demo days with middle- and high-school science teachers
  • Booths at STEM nights, libraries & community science fairs
  • A speaker series pairing students with working medical engineers and graduate researchers
